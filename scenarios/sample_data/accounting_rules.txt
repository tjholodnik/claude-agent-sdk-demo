ACCOUNTING RULES FOR P&L STATEMENT
===================================

1. REVENUE RECOGNITION
   - All rows with Type = "income" are recognized as revenue in the period they appear.
   - Revenue categories: Product Sales, Consulting Services, and any other income items.

2. COST OF GOODS SOLD (COGS)
   - Raw materials are classified as COGS (direct cost).
   - COGS is subtracted from Revenue to compute Gross Profit.

3. OPERATING EXPENSES (OPEX)
   - Office rent, Salaries and wages, Utilities, Marketing and advertising,
     Software subscriptions, Travel and entertainment are OPEX.
   - OPEX is subtracted from Gross Profit to compute Operating Income (EBIT).

4. REPORTING PERIODS
   - Group transactions by month (YYYY-MM).
   - Produce a summary row per month and an overall total.

5. CALCULATIONS
   - Gross Profit  = Total Revenue - COGS
   - Gross Margin  = Gross Profit / Total Revenue * 100  (as a %)
   - EBIT          = Gross Profit - Total OPEX
   - Net Margin    = EBIT / Total Revenue * 100           (as a %)

6. OUTPUT FORMAT
   - Print a formatted table with columns:
       Month | Revenue | COGS | Gross Profit | Gross Margin% | OPEX | EBIT | Net Margin%
   - Include a TOTAL row at the bottom.
   - Currency values should be formatted as  $X,XXX.XX
